Frequently Asked Questions

Android users may see notifications of apps trying to access their data on a daily basis. However, it's difficult for users to know what kind of data is being collected by those apps, and why the data is being collected.
The goal of our website is to educate users about what kind of network activity is being carried out by android apps. We present queryable data visualizations of 'where' and 'why' data is being sent.
Learn more about this project from our published paper.

PrivacyGrade focuses on analyzing smartphone apps, whereas Android Network Traces analyzes where our data is going and why. Android Network Traces also focuses more on the third parties that are gathering data about us.

The most common way for smartphone apps to communicate with cloud servers is through HTTP(S) requests. We built a custom VPN to intercept these outgoing HTTP(S) traffic requests.
Our entire github repo is public and can be accessed on github. You can also download the entire dataset we used for the analysis.

For each the key-value pair parsed from HTTP requests, we perform a taxonomy look-up to assign this request a data type. The taxonomy used is built by a group of researchers and students at Carnegie Mellon University. In this example, a request with the key being 'userAdvertisingID' will be categorized as AdvertisingID under the PhoneID datatype.

Based on the identified data type, we use a supervised machine learning algorithm to predict the possibility for each candidate purpose and output the most likely one. Our testing results show that algorithm can predict the data collection purpose with an average precision of 84% (among 19 unique categories).

Our predictative results are based on the one-time analysis of the dataset here. We assume the data type sent to the cloud services and the data collection purposes to be quite static. If we update the raw dataset in the future, we will put up a notice for update.

We only collected data from about 10,000 android apps. It's likely that the app your are looking for was added after our data collection process.
