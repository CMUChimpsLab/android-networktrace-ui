Many smartphone apps gather and send lots of data to various third-party services around the Internet. However, it's hard to know exactly what apps are sending out what data and why. Here, we show which of five data types are collected most by the apps. Browse the site to out find where the data goes.

How Did We Build This

Collecting and Analyzing Data

As a team of researchers at Carnegie Mellon University, we started this research in November 2016. We collected 6.3 million network traces from approximately 15 thousand apps using 8 phones, and analyze them into 5 categories of data types. We built a custom VPN to intercept the network traces from smartphone apps to cloud servers through HTTP(S) requests. Read more about our data collection process.

In this website, we present data visualizations showing “who” collects our data, “what” data they collected, “where” do they send our data to, and “why” the data is being sent. You can search to find apps on your phone and see how you can better protect your data.
